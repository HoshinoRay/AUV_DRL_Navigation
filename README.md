How to attain robust performance in complex and dynamic environment is one of the core research topics in robotics nowadays. It is also main challenge AUV(Automatic Unmanned Vehicle) face in order to perform underwater tasks and motion control. Reinforcement Learning is demonstrating its magical power in robotics training, however, main stream training platforms like Mujoco and Gym focuses on rigid body physic simulation, lacking the fluid dynamic consideration which is crucial in AUV simulation and training, leading to a dilemma in Sim2Real implementation. While the high precision underwater simulator like Stonefish and UUV simulator fails to support large scale RL training. In this experiment, we aim to take advantage of both and construct a reliable platform and AUV targeted RL method. Taking the collected data in stonefish as ground truth and compute the nonlinear fluid dynamic part by subtracting the known part such as gravity and buoyancy. Then input the data into a MLP deep neural network to train a model that predicts the component that are nonlinear and difficult to explicitly compute. Eventually combine the model with Mujoco physic simulator and apply main reinforcement algorithm like PPO to test the agent and the training platform.
